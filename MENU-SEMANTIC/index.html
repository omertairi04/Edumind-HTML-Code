Pizza history

Pizza is one of the most beloved dishes worldwide, known for its versatility and irresistible combination of flavors. Originating from Italy, it typically consists of a round, flat base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and a variety of other ingredients, such as meats, vegetables, and herbs, then baked at a high temperature.

The classic Margherita, named after Queen Margherita of Savoy, is a simple yet iconic variety topped with fresh tomatoes, mozzarella, and basil, representing the colors of the Italian flag. Beyond traditional Italian styles, pizza has evolved into countless regional adaptations.
